# Agile vs Waterfall

Status: Tested

## Statement
Agile is better suited for projects with changing requirements.

## Assumptions
- Requirements may change during development.
- Fast feedback is important.
- Iterative delivery improves learning.

## Supporting Evidence
- Agile supports short iterations and regular feedback.
- Waterfall works better when requirements are fixed from the beginning.
- Software engineering lectures compare Waterfall as linear and Agile as iterative.

## Counterarguments
- Waterfall can be easier to manage for simple projects with stable requirements.
- Agile requires active communication and discipline.
